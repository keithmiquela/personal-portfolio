About Me

Hi, I’m Keith — a Computer Science and Statistics student at Rutgers University with a passion for problem-solving, creative software development, and machine learning.

What first drew me to computer science wasn’t just coding — it was the thrill of solving problems and building things that make life easier or more inspiring. I’ve always loved figuring things out, whether it’s cracking algorithms on LeetCode or crafting a meaningful UI from scratch.

This summer, I’m diving into machine learning through Stanford’s CS229, working on two internships (data analytics + software dev), and learning full-stack development while building personal projects. I also stay active in student life as the Marketing Chair for the AI Club and VP of the Origami Club.

I’m currently focused on a career in machine learning engineering or AI-driven software development, with a long-term goal of possibly transitioning into product management or technical leadership. Wherever I go, I want to be at the intersection of tech, creativity, and real-world impact.

When I’m not coding, you can find me exploring new hiking trails, practicing origami, or playing video games with friends. I’m always up for a challenge, whether it’s in the code or on the trail.
